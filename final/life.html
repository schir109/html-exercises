“A little nonsense now and then, is relished by the wisest men.”

Roald Dahl was a spy, an ace fighter pilot and a medical inventor, but he’s probably best known as the author of stories like Charlie and the Chocolate Factory, Matilda, and The BFG. You can learn more at

roalddahl.com

September 13, 1916

Roald Dahl is born in Llandaff, Cardiff, Whales, UK.

1920

Dahl's older sister dies from an infection following a burst appendix. Shortly after, his father dies of pneumonia.

November 1939

Following the outbreak of World War Two, Dahl joins the Royal Air Force.

1940

While posted in Libya, Dahl is injured in a plane crash. He returns to the Air Force 6 months later.

July 1941

Roald Dahl returns to Britain.

1946

Dahl's first short story collection is published.

July 2, 1953

Roald Dahl marries Patricia Neal in New York.

1961

Publication of Roald's first famous book for children, James and the Giant Peach.

1964

Publication of Charlie and the Chocolate Factory.

1968

Chitty Chitty Bang Bang is released. Dahl cowrote the screenplay for the film with Ken Hughes.

1970

Publication of Fantastic Mr. Fox.

1971

Release of the first film adaptation of Willy Wonka and the Chocolate Factory.

1982

Publication of Revolting Rhymes and The BFG.

1988

Publication of Matilda.

November 23, 1990

Roald Dahl dies at the age of 74.

1991

Publication of Roald Dahl's final works; creation of Roald Dahl's Marvellous Children's Charity.

1996

Film versions of Roald Dahl's James and the Giant Peach and Matilda released.

September 13, 2006

The first ever Roald Dahl Day takes place.

2009

Release of Wes Anderson's Fantastic Mr. Fox.

April 2013

The musical adaptation of Roald Dahl's Matilda opens on Broadway.

2016

Release of Steven Spielberg's The BFG.
